Public Secure

ODS Lab, exploring single sign-on design

Public secure is a government team that leads the initiative to create single sign-on for online applications across the government of Ontario. As the consulting team, we introduced user-centric design by demonstrating the importance of research and synthesis, while developing discovery phase insights and prototype screens.

Understanding

establishing context

13 stakeholders were interviewed, which were all different program areas that have the potential to implement better single sign-on.

12 end users from the general public were interviewed.

Stakeholders

Key takeaways from analyzing the existing user flows:

  • login frustrations stem from confusing onboarding processes & a lack of hierarchy in the presentation of information.
  • relatively small application access frequency further accentuates frustration upon login.

In addition to understanding the problem space and creating a basis for further insight, stakeholder analysis yielded the following four themes to be examined in end users which in turn shaped the conversations regarding the possible approaches to single sign-on:

  • terminology comprehension
  • security perceptions
  • account attitudes
  • government participation

Synthesizing

generating guidance

Sam, Alex & Jamie stray from typical personas. They illustrate the principle of designing for generality, rather than pinpoint a specific user group the problem is being solved for. Thus, considering the edge cases was the overarching guidance we used to design for all. This was done by considering a variety of user flows and exploring feature sets of different user groups.

Prototyping and Evaluating

materializing and testing ideas

After conducting generative research, we mapped out our general understanding of what flows needed to be demonstrated in the discovery phase. The initial interpretation were these 4 core scenarios:

  • enrolment as a net new user
  • enrollment as returning single sign-on
  • logging in to application area
  • password reset

Some key changes made throughout several iterations:

Presentation of sign in options & transfer information :

It was important to strike a balance between presenting enough information for users who need it and not overwhelming the general majority.

The order of application enrollment

Ensuring that actions users are expecting, such as entering an enrollment pin first was essential to reducing frustration and friction, which was found throughout 12 usability tests.

Reimagining onboard architecture

This change allowed us to present sign-in options as true options, rather than what appeared to be separate account types, which was the decided product model after several generative discussions.

Personal Learnings

takeaways

  • Establishing higher level questions & analogies for mental models is super valuable to asking users the right generative questions.

  • Discovery phase projects give opportunity to dabble in lots of design processes in-depth, generative and evaluative.

  • Content design is a LOT more than just the effective words. Projects such as Public secure are dysfunctional without well thought out content and navigation. Although this might imply frustrating debates over subtle nuances, it’s well worthwhile!
