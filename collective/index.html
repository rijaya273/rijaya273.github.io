Collective

designing for mental health inclusion

Built in a brief 24 hour product design sprint, Collective is a community app built around mental health experiences. The key features were designed for fostering inclusion for those with mental health disabilities.

Investigation

Although we were constrained by time and resources, we conducted 7 generative interviews. Placing emphasis on designing for the barriers to inclusivity allowed us to not only focus on people with mental health difficulties. This converted into interviews with any and all users with thoughts on the state of the mental health-inclusion space.

Reflection

looking forward

By designing features as direct answers to pseudo-researched spaces, our proposed solution to the challenge prompt quite throughly explored one possible method to tackling exclusion of people with mental health disabilities. Taking a functinoal approach had it’s benefits in creating throughness in the solution, which was well suited to a 24 hour exercise.

With more time and resources, further exploration could have been done in other solutions spaces, possibly more organically for better idea development.

Furthermore, there was also room for improvement in the actual work done:

  • Accessibility: while visuals were well-intended for cleanliness, the soft pastels are likely inaccessible to a wider audience, which should have been considered from the beginning.

  • Integration: from a product thinking standpoint, it was easy to default to a standalone app due to the nature of the twenty-four hour exercise, further integration could be thought out to make Collective part of a greater, holistic solution.

Collective was a great exercise to go through in both understanding a problem space in as much depth and speed as possible, in addition to practicing quick execution of visual prototyping.
